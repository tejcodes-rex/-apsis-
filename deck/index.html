APSIS05 / 14
Technical depth · why the numbers are real

Foster 2D collision probability.

At closest approach the relative motion is linear. We collapse the 3D geometry onto the encounter plane perpendicular to the relative velocity, project the combined position covariance into that plane, and integrate the 2D Gaussian over the combined hard-body disk.

  • Direct polar quadrature, exact in the fine limit
  • The same method NASA CARA and ESA run operationally
  • Covariance model documented and shown in the UI, not hidden
// encounter-plane integral
Pc = ∬|x|≤R N(x; μ, C₂) dx

APSIS06 / 14
Technical depth · the autonomous decision

Minimum-propellant maneuver, anchored to truth.

  • Searches lead time × in-track Δv, the minimum-energy axis for separation
  • Bisects for the smallest burn that drives Pc below the safe target
  • Propagates the actual post-burn orbit with a universal-variable two-body solver
  • Differential correction against SGP4 cancels model error to first order
  • Reports Δv, lead time, propellant, before/after Pc, and explains itself

"...a 4.2 cm/s prograde in-track burn executed 95 min before closest approach widens the miss to 8.6 km and lowers probability to 9.1×10⁻⁷, a 1.2×10² reduction, at a propellant cost of 9.7 g for a 500 kg / 220 s-Isp bus."

, generated decision rationale, citing exact optimizer outputs
